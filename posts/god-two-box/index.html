God Ordains You to Two-Box

21 September 2025   205 words   ~1 min

Consider the following argument:

  1. If God exists then God would tell you to two-box.
  2. If God exists then everything God recommends to do is rational to do.
  3. Therefore, If God exists then God would tell you to two-box and everything God recommends to do is rational to do. (From 1 and 2),

(2) is surely uncontroversial, an all good, all powerful, and all knowing being tells me that that some action is rational I’m almost certain that it is. (1) seems to just be a variant of the well wishing friend argument George Schlesinger provides. If you have a friend that wants the best for you, knows the content of the opaque box, and they’re given the option to recommend what you should do, they’re always going to recommend you take both boxes. Now presumably God both wants the best for you and knows the content of the opaque box. So God would recommend two-boxing.

So either two-boxing is rational in Newcomb’s problem or God doesn’t exist. A corollary of this is that the various arguments for God (Modal ontological arguments, contingency arguments, fine-tuning arguments) are arguments for two-boxing and the various arguments for one-boxing (Why Aincha’ Rich?) are arguments for the non-existence of God.
